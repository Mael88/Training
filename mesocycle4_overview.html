Phase Details

Weeks 1–2 — Accumulation A

Jab and cross only — no hooks yet. Focus on stance, hip rotation, guard return after each strike. Intensity low-medium: technique must hold throughout every round.

Resist the urge to go hard. This is foundation work.

Week 3 — Accumulation B

Add lead and rear hook. Technique session introduces hooks in isolation first. Output session begins integrating into short 2–3 strike combinations. Volume stays similar, intensity ticks up slightly.

Weeks 4–5 — Intensification

Week 4: add front kick and roundhouse alongside punch combinations. Week 5: add knee strikes — push output, shorter rest, more rounds. HR targets rise into Z4–Z5 on output sessions.

Your legs will feel this. The combined load with lower calisthenics is intentional.

Week 6 — Taper

Volume drops ~30%, intensity stays. Use the full striking toolkit built across the cycle. Arrive at deload sharp, not beaten up.

Week 7 — Deload

No structured bag work. Optional: light shadowboxing only (no bag contact). Standard calisthenics deload as per CalisthenicsApp protocol.

Cervical Prehab Warm-Up · every session · 5 min
  • Chin Tucks
    2×10 reps, slow and controlled. Seated or standing.
  • Cervical Rotations
    5 reps each side. Pain-free range only — do not force end range.
  • Lateral Neck Stretch
    20–30 sec each side. Gravity only — do not pull with the hand.
  • Scapular Retractions
    2×15 reps. Unloads the cervical spine by activating mid-back.
  • Shoulder Rolls
    10 forward, 10 backward. Releases upper trapezius tension.

Judo Carryovers

Hip rotation is the engine — a cross is a hip rotation that travels through the fist. Same principle as your throws, different output direction.

Stance stability — your judo base and balance under pressure applies directly. Never be off-balance after a strike.

Kime — locking force at impact rather than pushing through. Strike through the target, snap back to guard.
