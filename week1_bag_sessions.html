Week 1 · Accumulation A

Session A — Technique

~45 min RPE 5–6 Z2–Z3 · 110–140 bpm
Two strikes only: jab and cross. This week is about laying the wiring correctly — stance, hip rotation, guard return. The cross is a hip rotation that travels through the fist instead of through a kuzushi. Same mechanic, different output direction.
Warm-Up

Cervical Prehab 5 min

  • Chin tucks — 2×10, slow and controlled
  • Cervical rotations — 5 reps each side, pain-free range
  • Lateral neck stretch — 20–30 sec each side, gravity only
  • Scapular retractions — 2×15 reps
  • Shoulder rolls — 10 forward, 10 backward

Movement Prep 3 min

  • Light shadowboxing — no power, find stance & footwork
  • 10 hip circles each side
  • 10 knee raises each side
  • 30 sec light bounce — find your rhythm
Main Work — 6 Rounds
3 min rounds · 90 sec rest · RPE 5–6 · Slow is smooth. Smooth is fast.
1
Stance & Jab — Slow and Clean
Focus
Jab only. Throw it slow, throw it clean.
Stance check: lead foot points at the bag, rear foot at ~45°, weight evenly distributed.
Jab mechanics: shoulder drives forward, hip stays square, fist returns immediately to guard.
Common error: dropping the rear hand when jabbing. Keep it glued to your cheek.
Jab × 3 → reset footwork → repeat. No rushing.
2
Jab + Footwork
Focus
Same mechanics as Round 1 — add lateral movement after each jab sequence.
After each sequence: take 2 steps sideways (either direction) and reset your stance before the next jab.
You are not a statue in front of the bag. Movement is part of the strike.
3
Cross — Hip Rotation Focus
Key Mechanic
Rear hip drives forward and rotates — this is your judo hip rotation. Same mechanic, different direction.
Rear heel: pivots off the ground as the hip turns. If the heel stays down, the rotation is blocked.
Wrist position: fist lands vertical or slightly angled — not horizontal (protects the wrist on impact).
Common error: pushing the cross instead of rotating into it. If you don't feel it in your hip and oblique, the rotation isn't happening.
Cross × 2 → reset → repeat.
4
Cross + Angle
Focus
Same cross mechanics as Round 3.
Add the pivot: after each cross, pivot 45° using your front foot.
This is the beginning of angle work. You are not just punching — you are changing position after the punch.
5
Jab-Cross — First Combination
First combination of the mesocycle
Principle: the jab is the door opener, the cross is the strike that matters. Let the jab land before you think about the cross.
Connection: the return of the jab should feed directly into the rotation for the cross.
Jab-cross → step back to safe distance → reset. One combination at a time.
6
Jab-Cross — Find the Rhythm
Focus
Same combination. Now let it flow slightly more naturally. Still RPE 5–6 — you are not going hard. You are finding the rhythm.
Focus: the connection between the two strikes feels natural, not mechanical.
By the end of this round, the combination should start to feel like yours.
Cool-Down

Recovery 5 min

  • Chest & shoulder stretch — 30 sec each side
  • Hip flexor lunge stretch — 45 sec each side
  • Upper trap stretch (lateral neck) — 30 sec each side
  • Breathing reset — 2 min nasal breathing, slow exhale

Post-Session Log

Coaching Notes

Cross check: Does it feel like a rotation or a push? Rotation = correct. Push = rear heel or hip not engaging.

Guard check: Is the rear hand dropping during the jab? Address it consciously before it becomes habit.

Combo check: Does the jab-cross feel like two separate events or one connected movement?

HR check: Session A should stay firmly in Z2–Z3. If you're hitting Z4, you're going too hard.

Week 1 · Accumulation A

Session B — Output

~45 min RPE 7 Z3–Z4 · 135–155 bpm
You know the mechanics from Tuesday. Today you apply them with intent. Every punch should mean something. Controlled aggression — technique must still hold.
Warm-Up

Cervical Prehab 5 min

  • Chin tucks — 2×10, slow and controlled
  • Cervical rotations — 5 reps each side, pain-free range
  • Lateral neck stretch — 20–30 sec each side, gravity only
  • Scapular retractions — 2×15 reps
  • Shoulder rolls — 10 forward, 10 backward

Movement Prep 3 min

  • Light shadowboxing — no power, find stance & footwork
  • 10 hip circles each side
  • 10 knee raises each side
  • 30 sec light bounce — find your rhythm
Main Work — 8 Rounds
2 min rounds · 60 sec rest · RPE 7 · Controlled aggression. Technique must hold.
1
Jab — 60% Speed
Warm the pattern up before pushing it. Jab sequences with footwork — same as Tuesday Round 2.
Focus: mechanics must be clean before speed goes up.
2
Jab — 80% Speed
Step up the speed and commitment from Round 1.
Guard check: rear hand stays at the cheek even as speed increases. This is where the error creeps back in.
3
Cross — 60% Speed
Reset to cross only. Build the rotation back up from 60%.
Feel the heel pivot at 60% — it's easier to notice at lower speed.
4
Cross — 80% Speed
Push the rotation. By 80%, the cross should feel like it has genuine weight behind it.
Hip check: if you're not feeling it in your oblique, the hip isn't fully rotating. Slow down and reset.
5
Jab-Cross — Controlled Aggression
RPE 7 zone. Combinations should be sharp and purposeful.
Between combinations: move, reset your feet, stay active. Don't stand still waiting for the next sequence.
The bag will swing — adjust your position to it. React, don't ignore it.
6
Jab-Cross — Push the Pace
Same combination. Slightly higher output than Round 5. Stay at RPE 7 — controlled, not maximum effort.
Check your guard return: as fatigue builds, the hands drop first. Fight that tendency.
7
Free Round
No prescribed pattern. Mix jabs, crosses, and jab-cross combinations as feels natural.
React to the bag. If it swings, adjust your angle. Use your footwork.
This is where the week's work should start showing up as something that feels like yours.
8
Free Round — Finish Strong
Same as Round 7. Push to the end of the round.
Final 30 sec: give everything you have left. Technique first, then output. End the session well.
Cool-Down

Recovery 5 min

  • Chest & shoulder stretch — 30 sec each side
  • Hip flexor lunge stretch — 45 sec each side
  • Upper trap stretch (lateral neck) — 30 sec each side
  • Breathing reset — 2 min nasal breathing, slow exhale

Post-Session Log

Coaching Notes

HR check: Session B should climb into Z3–Z4 by rounds 5–8. If it stays in Z2, increase your output.

Peak HR: Note your peak HR during this session — useful data as the cycle progresses.

Feedback for Week 2: After this session, take 2 minutes to note what felt natural, what felt forced, and where you noticed fatigue first. This will inform how Week 2 sessions are structured.
