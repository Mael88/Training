Kicks — Distance Reset Drill
CORRECTION
Fixing the Distance Problem
Mae geri and mawashi geri — both legs, 60% effort. Addresses the too-close problem from Week 4 Session B.
Distance reset step: before every kick, take one small step back to create kicking range. Make it deliberate — step back, set stance, then kick. You are training the habit of creating space before committing.
Lead mawashi geri — stay tall: drive the hip laterally, keep the torso upright. The power comes from the hip rotation, not from throwing body weight at the bag.
If you still feel too close for the kick after the reset step, you are not stepping far enough. The kick should reach the bag comfortably from the extended position.
3
Hiza Geri — Knee Strike Isolation
NEW
New — Hiza Geri (Knee Strike)
In atemi-waza: hiza geri — knee strike driven upward at close range. Used when distance has closed after punches or in a clinch. It is a short, explosive, linear weapon. Unlike kicks, the knee requires proximity, not distance.
Mechanics: drive the rear knee sharply upward toward the bag. The hip drives upward and forward — same hip extension principle as mae geri but abbreviated and more vertical. Supporting leg rises onto the ball of the foot.
Upper body: both hands stay at guard height. On the bag, grip the bag briefly with both hands and drive the knee in — this simulates the clinch application and adds the correct upper body engagement.
Impact: the point of the kneecap or just above it. Drive through the target.
Recovery: knee returns to the floor in fighting stance. No hopping or spinning.
Common error: the knee swings forward in a wide arc rather than driving straight up. Drive is vertical — think elevator, not pendulum.
Rear hiza geri ×3 → reset → lead hiza geri ×3 → reset → repeat. Slow and deliberate.
4
Hiza Geri — With Bag Grip
KNEE
Clinch Context
Same knee mechanics — now grip the bag with both hands before each knee strike.
Grip and pull: grab the bag at shoulder height. Pull it toward you as you drive the knee up. In atemi-waza you pull the opponent into the knee — they cannot create distance to defend. Deliver 2–3 knees per grip before releasing and resetting.
The grip-pull-knee sequence uses the same kumi-kata instinct from your judo gripping. Let that carry over — this should feel instinctive.
Grab bag → 2–3 hiza geri → release → step back to stance → reset → repeat.
5
Cross-Lead Hook + Hiza Geri
COMBO
First Knee Combination
Cross-lead hook (entering close range) → rear hiza geri. The two punches close the distance — the knee is the close-range finish.
Distance logic: the cross-hook naturally brings you inside kicking range. From that close distance a kick is difficult — but a knee is exactly right. This is why the knee exists here.
Transition: after the hook lands, weight is already forward and close. Drive the rear knee up directly from that position — no step back before the knee.
Cross-lead hook → rear hiza geri → step back to stance → reset → repeat.
6
Free Round — Full Toolkit, Controlled
Re-Entry Free Round
Use everything — punches, kicks, knees. No prescribed combinations. RPE 6–7 ceiling.
Distance awareness: vary your range consciously. Kicks at long range (with reset step), punches at medium range, knees after punches have closed the gap.
Three ranges, three weapon systems. You now have all of them. This is the full atemi-waza picture.

Distance Management — Key Correction
  • Before kicks: take a reset step back after punches. Punches close distance — you need to recreate kicking range before committing to a kick.
  • Before knees: no step back needed. After punches close the gap, knee from there directly.
  • After knees: step back sharply before the next entry. Do not linger at close range.
  • Lead mawashi geri lean: stay tall, drive the hip laterally. Power from hip rotation, not body momentum.
Universal Corrections — All Strikes
  • Shoulder blade down: cross and hooks — push the blade down and forward as each strike launches. Now automatic — maintain it.
  • Guard stays up during all strikes: punches, kicks, knees. Every combination ends with both hands at cheek height.
  • Supporting foot pivot on mawashi geri: non-negotiable. No pivot = no hip rotation = no power.
  • Chamber before every kick: quality of the kick is determined at the chamber. No shortcuts.
