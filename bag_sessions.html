Main Work — 5 Rounds
3 min rounds · 90 sec rest · RPE 6 · Introduce hooks slowly. No rushing.
1
Jab-Cross — Corrections Active
Foundation Check
Jab-cross only. Warm the existing mechanics before hooks arrive.
Shoulder depression cue: as the cross launches, push the shoulder blade DOWN and forward into the bag. Do not let the shoulder ride up. The elbow path corrects automatically when the shoulder stays depressed.
Hip reset: if throwing two crosses in a row, the heel returns to the floor between each one. Full rotation out, full recovery back.
2
Lead Hook — Isolation
NEW
New — Mawashi-Zuki Lead
In atemi-waza: mawashi-zuki with the lead hand. Horizontal arc. The engine is the lead hip snapping inward — same principle as tai-sabaki, applied forward and laterally.
Starting position: lead elbow lifts to shoulder height. Forearm horizontal. Arm forms an L-shape before the arc begins.
The movement: lead hip drives sharply inward as the arm swings in a horizontal arc. The hip fires first — the arm follows. Power comes from the hip snap, not the arm swing.
Shoulder depression: the shoulder stays DOWN throughout the arc. Do not shrug into the hook — this is what overloads the trap.
Guard: rear hand stays at cheek height throughout. After the hook, lead hand returns to guard immediately.
If the hook fatigues the shoulder quickly, the arm is swinging independently of the hip. Stop — feel the hip snap first, then let the arm follow.
Lead hook x2 → reset → repeat. Slow and deliberate.
3
Rear Hook — Isolation
NEW
New — Mawashi-Zuki Rear
Same mechanics as lead hook — but the rear hip drives the rotation. The rear heel pivots as in the cross, but the arc travels laterally rather than straight forward.
Key distinction from the cross: the cross drives straight forward. The rear hook arcs horizontally to the side. The hip rotation is similar but the trajectory is different — feel it.
Rear elbow: lifts to shoulder height before the arc. Same L-shape as the lead hook. Do not let the elbow drop below shoulder height during delivery.
Heel: same pivot as the cross. If the heel stays flat, the hip is blocked.
If the rear hook feels identical to the cross, the trajectory is wrong — it is becoming a wide cross rather than a horizontal arc. Slow down and focus on the lateral direction of the arc.
Rear hook x2 → reset → repeat. Compare the hip feel to the cross.
4
Lead Hook + Rear Hook — Alternating
NEW
Connecting Both Hooks
Alternate lead hook and rear hook. Slow and deliberate — feel the hip switch between rotations.
Hip alternation: lead hook = lead hip snaps inward. Rear hook = rear hip rotates forward-laterally. Between the two, there is a brief neutral reset. Do not rush it.
Guard: non-striking hand stays at cheek height throughout. Both hands return to guard between each hook.
Think of it as two separate throws from the same stance — each needs its own hip engagement. The rhythm is: hook → guard return → hip reset → hook again.
Lead hook → reset → rear hook → reset → repeat.
5
Cross + Lead Hook — First Integration
First Combination With Hook
Cross followed immediately by lead hook. Keep it at RPE 6 — feel the hip transition, not the power.
Connection: as the cross returns, the lead hip immediately loads for the hook. The return of the cross feeds into the hook rotation — same way the jab return fed the cross.
If the hook feels disconnected — like an afterthought after the cross — pause between them. Feel the hip load for the hook, then deliver it. Speed is not the goal this round.
Cross → lead hook → full guard return → step back → repeat.

2 min rounds · 60 sec rest · RPE 7–8 · Integrate hooks. Guard holds.
1
Jab-Cross — Re-Establish Base
Jab-cross only. 70% effort. Re-establish the mechanics before hooks enter.
Shoulder depression active: set the shoulders down before every cross. Do not let the introduction of hooks reset the corrections from last week.
2
Hook Warm-Up — Both Hands
HOOKS
Lead hook and rear hook in isolation. 60% effort. Warm the hook mechanics before combining.
Hip check: does each hook feel hip-driven or arm-driven? If arm-driven, slow down and reconnect before pushing the speed.
3
Jab-Cross-Lead Hook
COMBO
First 3-Strike Hook Combination
Structure: jab (setup) → cross (power) → lead hook (finish from the side).
Connection: as the cross returns, the lead hip immediately loads for the hook. Cross rotation bounces into hook rotation.
70% effort this round — feel the connection before pushing the speed.
If the hook feels disconnected from the cross, pause between them. Feel the hip load first, then throw the hook.
Jab-cross-lead hook → full guard return → step → repeat.
4
Jab-Cross-Lead Hook — Push to RPE 7
COMBO
Same combination. Build to RPE 7. Move between combinations.
Guard: as hooks are new, the tendency is to focus on the hook and forget the guard. After every combination — both hands back at cheek height before resetting.
The bag will swing more from the hook. Step to the side as it comes back, reset your angle, attack again from the new position.
5
Jab-Lead Hook — Atemi Entry Pattern
COMBO
Short Entry Combination
Jab directly into lead hook. No cross in between.
Atemi-waza intent: the jab disrupts posture and creates an opening. The hook comes around from the side and closes distance. In a grappling context, this combination sets up a clinch entry — the hook brings you in close and off to the side. Keep that spatial intent even on the bag.
Mechanics: as the jab returns, the lead hip immediately snaps for the hook. The faster the jab return, the faster the hook loads.
Jab-lead hook → step off the line → reset → repeat.
6
Cross-Rear Hook — Same Side Power
COMBO
Two Strikes, Same Hand, Two Angles
Cross (rear hand straight) → rear hook (rear hand horizontal arc). Two strikes with the rear hand, two different trajectories.
Hip mechanics: the cross uses the rear hip rotating forward. The rear hook rotates more laterally. Between them, the hip briefly resets — heel down, then pivots again for the hook.
Tactical logic: the cross creates a straight-line threat that forces a reaction — the rear hook comes around the outside of that reaction. Two angles of attack from the same side.
This is technically demanding. If the rear hook loses all hip engagement after the cross, separate the two strikes and rebuild the hip reset between them before recombining.
Cross → rear hook → guard return → step → repeat.
7
Free Round — Full Toolkit
No prescribed combination. Use everything: jab, cross, lead hook, rear hook — in whatever sequence feels natural and reactive.
One rule: every combination ends with both hands at guard height before you move or reset.
React to the bag. Move. Change angles. This is where the week's work should start feeling like a coherent striking game.
8
Free Round — Finish Strong
Same as Round 7. Push to RPE 8.
Final 30 sec: everything you have left. Guard up, hips active, move after every combination. End the week well.
