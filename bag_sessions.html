Session A — Technique

~45 min RPE 5–6 Z2–Z3 · 110–140 bpm
Week 1 Debrief
HR Flag
Peak HR of 178–180 bpm last week is near-maximum. Your RPE was accurate — the cardiovascular system is simply deconditioned after months off the bag and working harder than the effort feels. Normal. Will come down over 2–3 weeks. Trust RPE, prioritise recovery and sleep this week.
What Worked
Jab-cross combination felt natural by Session B. Cross mechanics improved clearly session to session. Energy sharp on both days.
What to Fix This Week
Elbow flaring on the cross (power leaking to shoulder). Push vs rotation self-check (heel lift). Guard dropping in 3+ strike combos under fatigue.
Same two strikes. Week 2 is consolidation — the mechanics become more automatic and we start building rhythm. Less thinking, more feeling. The elbow path on the cross is the technical priority this week.
Warm-Up

Cervical Prehab 5 min

  • Chin tucks — 2×10, head slides back, gaze stays level
  • Cervical rotations — 5 reps each side, pain-free range only
  • Lateral neck stretch — 20–30 sec each side, gravity only
  • Scapular retractions — 2×15 reps
  • Shoulder rolls — 10 forward, 10 backward

Movement Prep 3 min

  • Light shadowboxing — no power, find stance & footwork
  • 10 hip circles each side
  • 10 knee raises each side
  • 30 sec light bounce — find your rhythm
Main Work — 6 Rounds
3 min rounds · 90 sec rest · RPE 5–6 · Less thinking, more feeling
1
Jab — Shoulder Drive Isolation
Focus
Jab only. Reset the pattern cleanly before building pace.
Shoulder drive: the shoulder is the engine, the arm is a lever. Feel the shoulder initiating — not the arm pulling.
Return speed: the hand snaps back as fast as it extends. Think rubber band — out and back in one motion.
If the rear hand drops when jabbing, stop and reset. Do not train the error.
2
Jab — Guard Integrity Under Movement
Focus
Add lateral movement after each jab sequence, same as last week.
New focus: rear hand stays at cheek level during the entire movement sequence — jab, step, reset, guard check.
Between each sequence: mental guard check before throwing the next jab. Is the rear hand where it should be? Make it a habit now so it survives fatigue later.
3
Cross — Elbow Path Drill
Key Correction This Week
Cross only. 50% speed. This round is entirely about the elbow path.
Elbow cue: before each cross, set the elbow pointing at the floor. Keep it tracking downward as long as possible during extension — only fully open at the last moment of travel. If it flares outward early, power bleeds into the shoulder instead of the bag.
Heel self-check: after every single cross, pause and check — did the rear heel lift? If yes, you rotated. If no, the hip didn't fire. This is your diagnostic this week.
If impact registers mainly in the shoulder, the elbow flared. Slow down further and reset the path before continuing.
Cross × 2 → pause → heel check → repeat.
4
Cross — Build Speed on Correct Mechanics
Focus
Same elbow path as Round 3. Build to 70% speed.
Feel check: impact should register in the LD and oblique — not the shoulder. If you lose that feeling as speed increases, drop back to 50% and rebuild.
Lead hand cue: imagine holding a phone to your ear throughout the cross. That is the height the lead hand must stay at.
5
Jab-Cross — Guard Return Protocol
Combination + Guard Discipline
Jab-cross combination. One at a time, full guard return between each.
Three moments: jab lands → guard returns → cross launches. All three are equally important. The guard return is not a pause — it is an active reset that feeds the cross rotation.
The combination will feel slower than last week. That is correct. Guard integrity now, speed later.
Jab-cross → full guard return → step back → reset. Repeat.
6
Jab-Cross — Flow and Rhythm
Focus
Let the combination flow more naturally. Move between sequences — step after each combo, reset feet, attack again.
Quality marker: by the end of this round, the jab and cross should feel clearly different — jab is a setup (shoulder-driven, fast, light), cross is power (hip-driven, full rotation). Two different feelings, one connected movement.
If both strikes feel the same, you're pushing both. The jab should snap; the cross should rotate.
Cool-Down

Upper Body 4 min

  • Chest & shoulder stretch — doorway or bag post, 30 sec each side
  • Cross-body shoulder stretch — pull arm across chest at elbow, 30 sec each side
  • Tricep / lat overhead stretch — bend elbow overhead, pull gently, 30 sec each side
  • Wrist flexor stretch — arm extended palm up, pull fingers back, 20 sec each side
  • Wrist extensor stretch — arm extended palm down, press fingers down, 20 sec each side

Lower Body & Breathing 3 min

  • Hip flexor lunge stretch — low lunge, back knee down, 45 sec each side
  • Upper trap stretch — lateral neck, gravity only, 30 sec each side
  • Breathing reset — 2 min nasal breathing, 4 sec inhale / 6 sec exhale

Post-Session Log

Coaching Notes

HR trend: if peak HR begins to come down from last week's 178 bpm, that is adaptation happening. Even a 5–10 bpm drop is meaningful.

Elbow check: does the cross feel different from last week? If impact is now registering lower (LD, oblique) rather than in the shoulder, the elbow path is correcting.

HR target: Session A should stay in Z2–Z3. If you're hitting Z4, you're going too hard for a technique day.

Session B — Output

~45 min RPE 7 Z3–Z4 · 135–155 bpm
You've rebuilt the elbow path on Tuesday. Today you test whether it holds under output and fatigue. The guard in combinations is the second priority — this session specifically targets the drop pattern from last week.

  • 10 knee raises each side

Main Work — 8 Rounds
2 min rounds · 60 sec rest · RPE 7 · Guard obsession this session

Guard Drill — Active for This Entire Session

At the end of every combination and between every round: consciously check — are both hands back at cheek height? Make it obsessive today. It will become automatic by Week 3.

1
Jab — Warm Up the Pattern
Jab sequences with footwork. 60–70% effort. Mechanics first before output.
Focus: shoulder drive, rear hand guard, snap return. Clean the pattern up before pushing it.
2
Cross — Elbow Path at Output Speed
Cross only. 75–80% effort.
Test: can you hold the elbow path from Tuesday at higher speed? Most people lose it here.
If the shoulder is taking the load at 80%, drop to 60% and rebuild the path. Speed on bad mechanics is wasted.
3
Jab-Cross — Guard Focus
Combination at 70% effort. Single priority: return to full guard between every jab and every cross.
Do not rush the next strike until the guard is back. The combination will feel slower — that is correct.
Guard integrity first, speed second. Speed without guard is a habit you do not want.
4
Jab-Cross — Increase Output
Build to RPE 7. Full guard return after every strike.
Bag reaction: the bag will swing. Step to the side as it comes back, reset your angle, attack from a new position.
Move between combinations. You are not a static target.
5
Jab-Cross-Jab — First 3-Strike Combination
New this week
Structure: jab (setup) → cross (power) → jab (close and reset). The final jab re-establishes your guard and creates distance.
This is where last week's guard drop appeared. After the cross, the third strike must fire with the guard already returning — not after.
If the guard drops between the cross and the final jab, slow the entire combination down. Structure over speed.
Jab-cross-jab → step back → reset guard → repeat.
6
Jab-Cross-Jab — Build Pace
Same 3-strike combination. Push to RPE 7. Move after every combination.
Fatigue self-check: when tired, the first thing to go is the guard. When you feel fatigue hit this round — think "guard up", not "hit harder".
7
Free Round
No prescribed pattern. Mix jabs, crosses, and jab-cross-jab as feels natural.
Constraint: every combination ends with both hands at guard height before you move or throw again.
React to the bag. If it swings wide, step off the line, reset your angle, attack again.
8
Free Round — Finish Strong
Same as Round 7. Push to the end of the round.
Final 30 sec: give everything you have left. Technique first — guard up, elbow path, heel pivot — then output. End the session well.

Week 2 · Reference

Common Mistakes

Review during rest periods
Keep this tab open during sessions. The errors listed here are the ones most likely to appear when fatigue builds — check yourself against them between rounds, not just at the start.
Jab — Common Mistakes
MistakeWhat it looks likeFix
Dropping the rear hand Guard falls to hip or chest when jabbing Rear fist stays glued to cheek throughout — even at speed
Leaning forward Weight shifts forward, head goes over the front knee Shoulder drives, not the torso — stay balanced over both feet
Telegraphing with shoulder Rear shoulder dips before the jab launches Launch directly from guard — zero wind-up
Arm-only jab No shoulder rotation, pure arm extension Even a small shoulder drive gives the jab structure and reach
Slow hand return Fist lingers at extension Return is as fast as extension — rubber band, out and back
Cross — Common Mistakes
MistakeWhat it looks likeFix
Rear heel stays flat No pivot, hip rotation is blocked entirely Heel must lift and pivot — your self-check after every cross
Elbow flares out Fist travels in an arc, shoulder takes the impact Elbow points at the floor during extension — only opens at the last moment
Arm too far from body Power goes to shoulder, not LD and oblique Elbow tracks close to the body until final extension
Pushing not rotating Hip doesn't engage, cross feels heavy and slow Feel it in the oblique — if you don't, the hip didn't fire
Lead hand drops Guard opens as rear hand extends Imagine holding a phone to your ear — that height, throughout
Over-rotation Weight falls off front foot after impact Stop the rotation at the bag — don't chase the punch past it
Your Specific Corrections This Week
  • Elbow path on the cross: keep the elbow tracking toward the floor for as long as possible during extension. Power goes to LD and oblique — not the shoulder.
  • Heel self-check: after every cross, pause and check — did the rear heel lift? Yes = rotation happened. No = hip didn't fire.
  • Guard in 3-strike combos: the guard must return between every single strike. Return to full guard after the cross before launching the final jab.
  • Jab under fatigue: when tired, drive the shoulder even more consciously. The arm goes lazy first — the shoulder drive is what keeps the jab structural.
